Five key changes in U.S. - Cuba relations

by Rashmi Chugani

The U.S. embassy in Cuba has been closed for more than half a century…
Now that President Obama has re-established
diplomatic relations, what will change?

Cigars

American tourists visiting Cuba will be able to bring back
up to $100 worth of tobacco and alcohol…
…when previously, it was illegal to bring
any goods made in Cuba back to the States.

Sports

The U.S. and Cuba will both be freer to bond over
their passion for baseball…
…as Cuba’s pool of talented players will no longer be
prevented from signing up to Major League teams.

Travel

Cuban-Americans will find it much easier to get permission
to travel to the island…
…as U.S. officials will no longer need advance notification
if the visit is to see family in Cuba.

Technology

Currently only 5% of Cubans have access to the Internet…
….that could all change as American telecommunication firms
will be among those allowed to invest in Cuba.

Trade

It will soon become much cheaper to import new vehicles…
…which could sadly be the end of Cuba’s iconic cars.
These changes could allow Cuba to catch up with five decades of social change.
